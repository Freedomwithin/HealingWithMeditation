Meditation Techniques

Explore a variety of meditation practices to find what resonates with you.

Mindfulness Meditation

Mindful Breathing

Focus on the sensation of your breath as it enters and leaves your body. This technique helps anchor you in the present moment and calm the mind.

  1. Find a comfortable position.
  2. Close your eyes gently.
  3. Bring your attention to your breath.
  4. Notice the rise and fall of your chest or abdomen.
  5. When your mind wanders, gently redirect it back to your breath.

Good for: Beginners, stress reduction, anxiety management, improving focus.

Body Scan

Bring awareness to different parts of your body, noticing any sensations without judgment.

  1. Lie down comfortably.
  2. Close your eyes.
  3. Start with your toes and gradually move your attention up your body.
  4. Notice any sensations, such as tightness, tingling, or relaxation.
  5. Release tension with each exhale.

Good for: Body awareness, relaxation, releasing physical tension, pain management.

Walking Meditation

Bring mindful awareness to the experience of walking. Pay attention to the sensations in your feet and legs, the movement of your body, and the environment around you.

Good for: People who find it hard to sit still, connecting with nature, cultivating mindfulness in everyday activities.

Eating Meditation

Pay full attention to the experience of eating, savoring each bite, and noticing the flavors, textures, and smells of your food.

Good for: Developing a healthier relationship with food, increasing enjoyment of meals, improving digestion.

Loving-Kindness Meditation

Metta Meditation

Cultivate feelings of love and compassion towards yourself and others. Silently repeat phrases of loving-kindness, extending them to yourself, loved ones, and eventually all beings.

  1. Sit comfortably and close your eyes.
  2. Begin by directing loving-kindness towards yourself.
  3. Repeat phrases like, "May I be happy. May I be well. May I be safe. May I be peaceful and at ease."
  4. Gradually extend these feelings to loved ones, friends, acquaintances, strangers, and even difficult people.
  5. End by radiating loving-kindness to all beings everywhere.

Good for: Increasing empathy, reducing anger and resentment, fostering positive emotions, improving relationships.

Other Techniques

Transcendental Meditation (TM)

Silently repeat a mantra (a sound, word, or phrase) to help the mind settle into a state of deep rest and inner stillness. Typically taught by certified TM instructors.

Good for: Stress reduction, deep relaxation, improving clarity and focus, accessing inner peace.

Vipassana Meditation (Insight Meditation)

Develop insight into the true nature of reality through close observation of physical and mental sensations. Pay meticulous attention to the arising and passing of sensations without judgment or reactivity.

Good for: Deepening self-awareness, understanding the impermanent nature of things, cultivating equanimity, liberation from suffering.

Zazen (Sitting Meditation)

The primary practice in Zen Buddhism. Typically involves sitting in a specific posture and focusing on the breath or simply maintaining awareness of the present moment without a specific object of focus.

Good for: Cultivating discipline, developing present moment awareness, experiencing inner silence, spiritual growth.

Yoga Meditation

Integrates physical postures (asanas), breathing exercises (pranayama), and meditation to promote physical, mental, and spiritual well-being.

Good for: Improving flexibility and strength, calming the mind, increasing body awareness, promoting overall health.

Guided Meditation

A teacher or recording guides you through the practice, often using imagery, visualizations, and affirmations.

Good for: Beginners, relaxation, stress reduction, achieving specific goals (e.g., improving sleep, reducing anxiety)
